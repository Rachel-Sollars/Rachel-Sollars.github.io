L-system OpenGL

Click here for repo link.

In second year I took an optional module focused on graphics. For it I developed this application which generates, constructs, and displays semi-random L-systems out of 3D cylinders. This project taught me lots about the graphics pipeline. Although I struggled with the complexities of vertex knitting and rendering in openGL, graphics and shaders are an area of programming that I want to improve at.
In this game, which was developed in Unity, I was in charge of the player character movements, as well as the traps and their functionality. The player movement was implemented using a CharacterController rather than a Rigidbody, which gave us incredible control over the game feel, but led to reduced options for traps, as the physics was not properly affecting the character. I have learned from this the importance of looking into all methods for solving a problem so as to be informed as to their pros and cons.
